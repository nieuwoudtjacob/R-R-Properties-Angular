SOUTH AFRICAN COUNCIL FOR THE PROPERTY VALUERS PROFESSION

HOSTING OF THE PRACTICAL WORKSCHOOL FROM 21 – 25 AUGUST 2023

Registration is now open and it is the first post Covid 19 practical work school that will be hosted as normal a contact session.

  1. Who may attend the Work school
    1. A Candidate Valuer (including a Candidate Single Residential Property Assessor – CSRPA) in his/her final. year of study of an accredited valuation qualification and who will be graduating in 2024.
    2. A Candidate Valuer who has completed his/her valuation qualification and wishes to sit for the Council’s admission examination to become a professional.
    3. A Candidate Single Residential Property Assessor (CSRPA) who will attend fewer sessions of the work school and expected to take test on the Time Value of Money (TVM).
    4. A Professional Associated Valuer or Professional Valuer who is already registered with the Council and wants to refresh his/her valuation knowledge and earn CPD points.
    5. A Candidate Valuer or a Professional Associated Valuer in preparation for the Board examination which was previously referred to as the pre-exam session.
    6. A delegate attending the practical work school as a requirement for the Board examination who will obtain a Certificate of Attendance.
    7. Any unregistered person ie a person not registered with the Council.


    Please note: It is compulsory that a delegate must attend the practical work school as a requirement to sit for the Board examination (to become professionals).



  2. The Tests
    1. Two (2) tests will be written by attendees at the work school.
    2. The first test will be written on the 3rd day ie on 23 August 2023 on the Time Value of Money (TVM). This test is compulsory for all registered persons only.
    3. The second test will be structured in a manner that it will test the attendees on all work presented at the work school. This test will be written on the last day of the work school being 25 August 2023.

  3. Allocation of CPD points
    1. CPD points will be allocated to those attending the work school, registered as professional associated or professional valuer but not candidate valuers. For those attending the work school as a refresher course will not receive CPD points.

RESEARCH SURVEY:


Hannah Mago, is a Master of Science in the Built Environment (Property Economics and Valuation) student at Nelson Mandela University, who is conducting research on the impact of technology adoption on property valuation in the residential property market in South Africa. The survey received ethics approval from Nelson Mandela University Research Ethics Committee. The ethics approval number is H22-ENG-CMA-020. Please find the attached ethics approval letter.

The research is significant as it will cover the knowledge gap by investigating how property technologies can mitigate the challenges faced by the property valuation sector in South Africa. By identifying the benefits and barriers of adopting property technology in the valuation of residential properties, property valuation firms, banks and insurance companies are provided with important insights that they can use to inform policy and practice regarding the adoption of Property Technology to reduce the property valuation disparities.

DOCTORATE RESEARCH PROJECT:


RUDO MELISSA ZHANDA is pursuing a Doctorate at Henly Business School, University of Reading in the United Kingdom. Her research project aims to investigate the awareness and the perception of value of professional bodies in the built environment. Empirical research on this subject is non-existent which is the need for this research.

Responses are anonymous and individual respondents will not be identified in the final report. The project has been subject to ethical review and has been given a favourable ethical opinion for conduct by the University of Reading.

BUILT ENVIRONMENT PRIVATE PRACTICE SURVEY


The objective of the CBE built environment professional private practices survey is to create a database for the purposes of creating information baseline to equip the CBE when engaging with relevant stakeholders such as government and private sector on matters impacting the built environment professions.

This database is not intended to create any expectations, but rather to assist the CBE in understanding issues that affect the built environment and its professions.

Your participation in this survey will equip the CBE with factual information to support its advocacy for change in the implementation of procurement policies and programmes intended to bring about transformation in the industry.

You are kindly requested to complete the survey by clicking here, and to circulate this link (https://forms.gle/52uLcqgt8VwRnJz19) to all BE private practices that you are aware of to complete the survey.
