APPOINTMENT OF DISCIPLINARY TRIBUNAL: PRO FORMA PROSECUTOR

APPLICATIONS ARE INVITED FROM SUITABLY QUALIFIED AND EXPERIENCED CANDIDATES FOR APPOINTMENT AS PR FORMA PROSECUTOR OF THE STATUTORY DISCIPLINARY TRIBUNAL OF THE SOUTH AFRICAN COUNCIL FOR THE PROPERTY VALUERS PROFESSION (SACPVP)

CLOSING DATE: 29 February 2024

The SACPVP has the statutory mandate to appoint a Disciplinary Tribunal in terms of Section 32 (2) of the Property Valuers Profession Act, 2000 (Act No. 47 of 2000) to hear a charge of improper conduct against a registered person, if a registered person charged denies the charge or fails to comply with Section 30 (3) (b) of the aforesaid Act. The SACPVP is obligated by its enabling Legislation to hear Disciplinary Tribunals in terms of Section 33 of the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa (Act No. 108 of 1996).

The Disciplinary Tribunal consists of three members. Legislation further prescribes that one member must be a person qualified in law with appropriate experience. This member will be appointed as the Pro Forma Prosecutor of the Disciplinary Tribunal.


The duties and the scope of work of a Pro Forma Prosecutor of the Disciplinary Tribunal include, but not limited to:

  1. Peruse the Investigation Report and draft the charges against a registered person.
  2. Ensure all disciplinary hearings are conducted in accordance with Section 33 of the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa.
  3. Ensure that all Disciplinary Tribunals are decided, with fully motivated reasons, within a reasonable time from the date of their hearing.
  4. Attend hearings and in terms of Section 32 (7) of the Property Valuers Profession Act, 2000.

The nature of the appointment requires that the applicant demonstrates leadership qualities, integrity, independence, dedication, good communication skills, analytical ability and independent judgement. The applicant must further possess and demonstrate the following academic qualifications, experience and competencies:

  1. Bachelor of Law degree.
  2. Eight (7) years post-admission experience as an Attorney or Advocate.
  3. Knowledge of Constitutional and Administrative Law.
  4. Understanding of the Property Valuers Profession Act, 2000 and the Legislation that governs the Councils in the Built Environment (BE), including the Council for the Built Environment Act, 2000 (Act No. 43 of 2000) (the CBE Act).

Remuneration is at a rate of R1500.00 per hour, excluding Value Added Tax (VAT). In addition, travel expenses will be reimbursed. The appointment will be for a period of three (3) years; and appointees will be utilised on a rotational basis.


Applications must include:

  1. Motivation justifying the applicant’s suitability.
  2. Comprehensive curriculum vitae (CV).
  3. Certified copies of identity document and qualifications.
  4. At least two (2) copies of ruling/judgment handed down where the applicant has served as a presiding officer.

Suitably qualified candidates are required to submit their applications to: legal@sacpvp.co.za

CLOSING DATE: 29 February 2024

The SACPVP has the statutory mandate to appoint a Disciplinary Tribunal in terms of Section 31 (1) of the Property Valuers Profession Act, 2000 (Act No. 47 of 2000) to hear a charge of improper conduct against a registered person, if a registered person charged denies the charge or fails to comply with Section 30 (3) (b) of the aforesaid Act.
